CloudVault

Introduction

CloudVault, a data storage provider, has reported a technical disruption impacting access to certain client files. The company is working with cybersecurity experts to restore services and ensure the integrity of stored data. While investigations continue, CloudVault insists that customer security remains its top priority and that affected businesses are being kept informed.

As part of the review, CloudVault has engaged a cybersecurity analyst to conduct a security assessment of its systems. The analyst will identify potential vulnerabilities and recommend appropriate measures to enhance security. The analyst will submit a report outlining the findings and recommendations.

Overview of the situation

CloudVault, a data storage provider for multiple businesses, suffered a catastrophic ransomware attack that locked critical business and customer files. The attackers exploited a zero-day vulnerability in the cloud infrastructure, allowing them to encrypt all company and client data. A Bitcoin ransom was demanded in exchange for a decryption key.

The breach was compounded by poor cybersecurity policies—CloudVault had no network segmentation, meaning attackers moved laterally across all connected systems. Additionally, a former employee’s account had not been deactivated, providing another attack vector.

Sensitive HR and legal documents were leaked onto the dark web, and the attackers publicly defaced the company website. A major law firm representing clients affected by the breach has started an investigation into CloudVault’s compliance failures under GDPR and data protection laws.

Cover Story

CloudVault is downplaying the attack, stating that a “temporary service outage” occurred due to unexpected system maintenance. They insist that "customer data integrity remains a priority", while secretly negotiating with cyber insurance providers about whether to pay the ransom.

Meanwhile, internal chaos is unfolding—executives are scrambling for legal cover, the CISO (Chief Information Security Officer) has resigned, and regulators are closing in. Clients are furious and threatening lawsuits. The ICO is investigating, and fines could reach millions.
